Health Recommendations for High Blood Pressure

Here are your personalized recommendations for managing your blood pressure!

Recommended Diet

  • Reduce sodium intake by limiting processed foods and using less salt in cooking. Choose low-sodium options where possible.
  • Increase potassium-rich foods like bananas, spinach, sweet potatoes and tomatoes to help balance sodium levels in the body.
  • Eat more whole grains, such as brown rice, oats and whole wheat bread, to improve heart health and regulate blood pressure.
  • Include heart-healthy fats from sources like olive oil, avocado and nuts, which help reduce inflammation and support healthy blood vessels.
  • Consume lean proteins such as skinless chicken, fish and legumes, and limit red meat and high-fat dairy products.

Recommended Exercise

  • Engage in regular aerobic exercises like walking, jogging, cycling, or swimming for at least 150 minutes per week.
  • Incorporate strength training exercises 2-3 times a week to improve cardiovascular health and muscle tone.
  • Try yoga or tai chi to help lower blood pressure and reduce stress levels, which can contribute to hypertension.
  • Take short walks throughout the day, especially if you sit for long periods, to improve circulation and reduce blood pressure.

Recommende Lifestyle Changes

  • Monitor your blood pressure regularly and aim to keep it within the healthy range recommended by your healthcare provider.
  • Maintain a healthy weight by balancing diet and exercise. Even small weight loss can help reduce blood pressure significantly.
  • Limit alcohol intake to no more than one drink per day for women and two for men, as excessive alcohol can raise blood pressure.
  • Quit smoking and avoid exposure to secondhand smoke to prevent further damage to your heart and blood vessels.
  • Practice relaxation techniques such as deep breathing, meditation, or progressive muscle relaxation to help reduce stress and lower blood pressure.
