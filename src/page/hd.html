Health Recommendations for Heart Disease

Here are your personalized recommendations for managing your heart health!

Recommended Diet

  • Choose heart-healthy fats like olive oil, avocado and nuts instead of saturated fats found in butter and fried foods.
  • Incorporate more fiber-rich foods like oats, lentils and vegetables to help lower cholesterol levels.
  • Eat lean proteins such as skinless chicken, turkey and fish like salmon to support heart health.
  • Limit salt intake to control blood pressure. Use herbs and spices like garlic and turmeric for flavor instead of salt.
  • Consume antioxidant-rich fruits like berries, apples and citrus fruits to help reduce inflammation in the body.

Recommended Exercise

  • Engage in at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week, such as brisk walking or cycling.
  • Incorporate strength training exercises 2-3 times a week to improve heart health and build muscle.
  • Practice relaxation exercises such as yoga or tai chi to reduce stress, which can affect heart health.
  • Consider swimming, which is a low-impact exercise that can improve cardiovascular fitness.

Recommended Lifestyle Changes

  • Monitor your blood pressure regularly and aim to keep it within a healthy range.
  • Quit smoking and avoid exposure to secondhand smoke, as smoking is a major risk factor for heart disease.
  • Limit alcohol intake to no more than one drink per day for women and two drinks for men.
  • Maintain a healthy weight by eating a balanced diet and exercising regularly to prevent additional strain on your heart.
  • Get adequate rest; aim for 7-9 hours of sleep each night to support overall heart health.
